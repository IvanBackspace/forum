Health Minister Mikhail Murashko spoke about the latest changes in the treatment of oncological diseases in the country over the past 4 years. He spoke on these issues on February 4 at the award ceremony of the "We Will Live!" prize in the Kremlin.

H3 Title

Murashko recalled that in 2018, Vladimir Putin gave instructions in his address to the Federal Assembly on the development of systems for providing care to cancer patients. And now, as part of the re-equipment, all modern methods of therapy are available in each subject of the Russian Federation, including nuclear therapy, immunobiological drugs and minimally invasive surgery.

Image taken from google.com

In 2018, in his address to the Federal Assembly, the President of Russia gave instructions on the development of this area. And literally yesterday, four new oncology dispensaries were opened. These are the pearls of healthcare, which, in fact, are created once in a century - such powerful medical organizations that in the coming years will fight and help people in the fight against these diseases. — Mikhail Murashko, Minister of Health

Another area was the creation of pharmaceutical production and medicines.

"Today, more than 100 drugs are in clinical trials and more than 130 domestic drugs were registered last year. All this ultimately allows us to have greater financial and physical accessibility, to no longer depend on some factors and thereby create conditions for combating this disease," the minister explained.

According to the ministry, Russia has great export potential in the field of nuclear medicine and the development of the Healthcare program will consolidate these positions.

All this, according to Murashko, will increase the availability of treatment for patients and reduce the risks from external factors.
